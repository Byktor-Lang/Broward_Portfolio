Teacher Guide — Semana 2: Spanish I bridge — personal information

Aug. 17–21 Spanish II · Miramar HS · Sr. Azuaje Modes: Interpersonal + Presentational (writing) 3 blocks (~90 min)

Week goal: Exchange personal details, likes, interests; write a Todo sobre mí profile. Spiral targets: Week 1 classroom language + question words. Interleaving status: ser, tener (Block 1) and gustar, estar (Block 2) are introduced blocked this week — the ser/estar contrast is Week 3, so do not juxtapose them yet. Reflection in: the Week 1 TBLT's reflection is imported into Block 1, replacing its exit ticket (a short formal-email reflection on the Week 1 survey). Assessment: profile + mingle pattern (Block 3). One task.

Bloque 1 — Soy y tengo (who I am)

Objective: I can say who I am and what I have.

Mode: Presentational + Interpersonal

Sequence + timing + volume (deep arc — 88 min)
1.Do-Now — spiral (Wk 1) (8) — 4 items: answer four question-word questions about yourself.
2.Input + CFU (15) — ser (soy/eres/es) + tener (tengo/tienes/tiene), blocked; ≥3 modeled each + 2 CFUs.
3.Guided pass 1 — conjuga (15) — 6-item set: choose soy/eres/es or tengo/tienes/tiene.
4.Communicative core — Encuentra a alguien que… (18) — ≥6 exchanges: find classmates matching 6 ser/tener facts; frames provided.
5.Brain break (3)
6.Guided pass 2 — interleave (12) — 6-item contextual fill mixing ser/tener + a spiraled question word; harder than pass 1.
7.Extended output — ¿Quién soy? (12) — 4–5 sentences: who you are (soy) + what you have (tengo).
8.Reflection (post-task, replaces exit ticket) (~12) — from the Semana 1 TBLT: short formal-email reflection on last week's class survey. Register shift hola → buenos días / formal close + signature; reflection sentences in English by design (Novice-Mid fence).

Extension (Reto extra): Add a sentence with tengo que (I have to…).

Why: ser and tener are anchored first, alone, so they encode before any mixing. Lens: the spiral Do-Now hooks with Week 1; Encuentra a alguien que… is the mid-block spike (movement + real info-gap); challenge varies conjugate → mingle → interleave → write; the ¿Quién soy? paragraph is the payoff.

Materials: Lesson §Bloque 1; “Encuentra a alguien que…” grid; primary-source QR (short authentic self-intro clip — attach your own).

Key — Bloque 1
Do-Now (spiral, sample): Me llamo… · Soy de… · Tengo … años · Me gusta…
CFUs: (1) “ser or tener for age?” → tener (Tengo 14 años). · (2) “Which for origin?” → ser (Soy de…).
Guided pass 1 (conjuga): 1. Yo soy estudiante. · 2. Yo tengo catorce años. · 3. ¿Tú eres de Florida? · 4. Mi amiga tiene el pelo largo. · 5. Él es simpático. · 6. Yo tengo una hermana.
Guided pass 2 (interleave): 1. ¿Cuántos años tienes? · 2. Yo soy de Miami y tengo dos hermanos. · 3. ¿De dónde eres tú? · 4. Mi profesor es paciente. · 5. Ella tiene el pelo corto y es simpática. · 6. ¿Cómo te llamas?
Output (sample): Soy Ana. Soy de Miami. Tengo catorce años. Tengo dos hermanos. Soy estudiante de español.
Reflection (post-task — replaces exit ticket): reflection lines (2, 4, 5) are open — accept any honest, on-topic English answer. Sample email: Buenos días, 1. Me llamo Ana. · 2. "…keep walking to many different classmates instead of getting everything from one person." · 3. Name: Diego · de Miami · 14 años. · 4. "Most classmates are from Florida." · 5. "…survey a new class to learn their names and where they are from." Close: Adiós. Me llamo Ana. Spanish stays in-fence (hola, buenos días, adiós, me llamo, de, años); no gustar/"likes".
Reflection — facilitation & fence note
Genre: formal_email · register shift: informal_to_formal_verb_phrase (frozen at the Semana 1 Gate A). Realized in-fence at the open (hola → buenos días) and close (walk-away adiós → adiós + me llamo signature). Honest-constraint note: a true verb-level formal shift (tú → usted) is out-of-fence for this Week-1 16-item PVS, so the shift is kept minimal by design, not breached. Run it (~12 min): ~2 min frame the register table · ~8–10 min write the email frame · ~2–3 min two or three volunteers read their opening line + one class pattern aloud.
Bloque 2 — Me gusta y estoy (likes & feelings)

Objective: I can say what I like and how I feel.

Mode: Interpersonal

Sequence + timing + volume (deep arc — 88 min)
1.Do-Now — spiral (Bloque 1) (8) — 4 items: a soy sentence, a tengo sentence, answer ¿De dónde eres?, one trait.
2.Input + CFU (15) — me gusta / me gustan + estar for feelings (estoy/estás/está), blocked; ≥3 modeled each + 2 CFUs.
3.Guided pass 1 — escoge (15) — 6-item set: me gusta vs me gustan, plus an estar feeling.
4.Communicative core — entrevista (18) — ≥6 exchanges: 2 likes, 1 dislike, how they feel in 3 classes; frames provided.
5.Brain break (3)
6.Guided pass 2 — interleave (12) — 6-item fill mixing gustar + estar + tener (Bloque 1); harder.
7.Extended output — gustos y sentimientos (12) — 4–5 sentences: 2 likes/dislikes + how you feel in 2 classes.
8.Exit ticket (5) — 3 items: one me gusta, one me gustan, one estoy sentence.

Extension (Reto extra): Say something you like and something you don't (no me gusta).

Why: estar is introduced for feelings only — its contrast with ser is deliberately held for Week 3, so keep them in separate blocks today. Lens: the spiral Do-Now hooks; the interview is the spike; challenge varies choose → speak → interleave → write; the gustos paragraph is the payoff and feeds Block 3's profile.

Past (recognition only): if it comes up, gloss me gustó = “I liked it” in a model line — do not ask students to produce past tense (calendar: past = recognition in model only).

Materials: Lesson §Bloque 2; interview partners.

Key — Bloque 2
Do-Now (spiral, sample): Soy simpático/a. · Tengo dos hermanos. · Soy de Miami. · Soy trabajador/a.
CFUs: (1) “one book vs many books — gusta or gustan?” → gusta / gustan. · (2) “feeling right now — which verb?” → estar (estoy).
Guided pass 1 (escoge): 1. Me gusta el fútbol. · 2. Me gustan las clases de arte. · 3. Me gusta la música. · 4. Me gustan los deportes. · 5. Yo estoy contento/a hoy. · 6. ¿Cómo estás tú?
Guided pass 2 (interleave): 1. Me gustan los libros de aventura. · 2. Hoy yo estoy cansado/a. · 3. Yo tengo dos clases de ciencia. · 4. No me gusta la tarea. · 5. Mi amigo está nervioso antes del examen. · 6. ¿Te gustan los deportes?
Output (sample): Me gusta la música. Me gustan los deportes. No me gusta la tarea. Estoy contento/a en la clase de arte. Estoy cansado/a en la clase de matemáticas.
Exit ticket: any correct me gusta + me gustan + estoy sentence.
Bloque 3 — Task block: interests mingle (TBLT)

Objective: I can write a short profile and find classmates with a shared interest.

Mode: Interpersonal + Presentational (writing)

Sequence + timing — TBLT nesting
1.Do-Now — spiral (Bloque 2) (8) — one me gusta sentence + one feeling (estoy…).
2.Pre-task (~17) — from the imported TBLT: write the Todo sobre mí profile (collect: writing sample).
3.Main-task (~63) — from the imported TBLT: mingle → find shared interests, report one class pattern. Replaces input/practice/output. Duration-sized to fill the block.
4.Reflection — not here: opens Semana 3 · Bloque 1, replacing that block's exit ticket.
TBLT slot — this week's task
Drop the Week 2 TBLT (interests mingle → class pattern) into tblt-imports/week-02/. The Engine reskins its pre-task + main-task into this block and never alters the task content. Request ~80 min (pre ~15–20 · main ~60–65). No file present in this sample — this is the empty slot.

Extension (Reto extra): A follow-up question you asked during the mingle.

Why: Week 2's task is the mingle → report-a-pattern (calendar). The Engine owns Blocks 1–2 (ser/tener, gustar/estar) and hands the task block to the imported TBLT — one major task cycle.

Materials: Lesson §Bloque 3; the imported TBLT from tblt-imports/week-02; collected profiles; primary-source QR.

Criteria — Bloque 3
Profile + mingle (calendar criteria): gives details (name, age, origin, ≥2 likes) · asks a follow-up during the mingle · reports one class pattern. Update the Class-Progress Record: note ser/estar readiness before the Week 3 contrast beat.
After the week
Differentiation (Weekly Approval): universal supports (word banks, frames) are in the Lesson. Decide whether any 504/IEP need calls for a Lesson Variant.
Class-Progress Record: did ser and estar encode separately? Week 3 Block 1 is the contrast beat — flag any class still shaky on either one so it spirals sooner.
