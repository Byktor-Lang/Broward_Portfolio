01

A typical bloque — and how a week builds

Same shape every block; new language every block. Ninety minutes is long enough to go deep — and long enough to lose a novice, so the arc is built to move.

  1. 1Do-Now — spiral warm-upRetrieval practice mixing the last bloque with material from weeks back. Spacing, not cramming, makes language last.~8 min
  2. 2Input + checks for understandingComprehensible presentation of the bloque's target (e.g. ser vs. estar), with frequent quick checks.~15 min
  3. 3Guided practice, pass 1Scaffolded, controlled practice — word banks and sentence frames so every student can start.~15 min
  4. 4Communicative coreThe heart of the bloque: students talk. Mingles, partner interviews, info-gaps — real meaning, not fill-in-the-blank.~18 min
  5. 5Brain breakA deliberate reset at the midpoint. On a 90-minute block this is not a luxury.~3 min
  6. 6Guided practice, pass 2 — interleavedThe same structure mixed with older material, so students must choose rather than repeat.~12 min
  7. 7Extended outputStudents write or speak several sentences of their own — the payoff the block has been building toward.~12 min
  8. 8Exit ticketA quick check so I know who has it and who needs reteaching next block.~5 min
  9. +Reto extraA self-directed challenge tied to the bloque's goal, ready for early finishers.any

How a week builds across three bloques

  • Bloque 1Meet a new structure on its own — blocked practice (e.g. ser + tener).
  • Bloque 2Meet the next structure, then interleave it with Bloque 1 so students must discriminate.
  • Bloque 3Task block: a real communicative task with an information gap the students have to close.
  • Next weekThe task's reflection opens the following week's Bloque 1 — spaced by days, not minutes.

What I believe about how students learn

  • Predictable structure, rotating content. Routine lowers anxiety for novices; variety keeps them engaged.
  • Teach for durable retention. Spacing, retrieval, and interleaving build language students keep — even when it makes today harder.
  • Comprehensible input, then real communication. Understand messages, then use them for a purpose — organized around the ACTFL modes.
  • Meet students where they are. Universal supports in every lesson; difficulty calibrated so challenge stays productive, never defeating.

03

The first three weeks, bloque by bloque

Each week is one arc across three blocks: introduce → interleave → task. Every bloque below is a real, print-ready student handout with a matching teacher guide.

Week 1Aug 10–14 · 3 bloques

Course launch & proficiency routines

Can-do: Use classroom Spanish, ask for help, and complete a baseline speaking & writing sample.

Task: class survey → class profile (3-part TBLT)Product: diagnostic writingInterpersonal + PresentationalView full lesson →
  • Bloque 1

    Saludos y frases de supervivencia

    Puedo saludar, presentarme y pedir ayuda en español.

    Do-Now: activación (4)→Input: 8-phrase survival bank + CFU→Guided 1: situación→frase (6)→Mingle (≥6 exchanges)→Guided 2: clasifica (8)→Output: auto-presentación (4–5 sent.)→Exit (3)
  • Bloque 2

    Palabras interrogativas e información personal

    Puedo hacer y contestar preguntas básicas sobre mí.

    Do-Now: spiral B1 (4)→Input: 6 question words + CFU→Guided 1: completa la pregunta (6)→Entrevista (≥6 exchanges)→Guided 2: empareja · interleave B1+B2 (7)→Output: Todo sobre mí (5 sent.)→Exit (3)
  • Bloque 3task

    Tarea: encuesta de clase → perfil de la clase

    Puedo compartir información sobre mí y contribuir al perfil de la clase.

    Do-Now: spiral B2→Pre-tarea (~17 min): recall · odd-one-out · build the question→Tarea principal (~63 min): info-gap class survey → class profile
Why: Survival language is the highest-leverage content of the year — it keeps the room in Spanish from day one, and the week collects a baseline writing sample that sets the first difficulty ceiling. The full three-part task: a pre-task primes the three survey questions, then in the main task each classmate may give you only one answer — so students must keep circulating, and nobody can finish the class profile alone. The reflection opens Week 2 · Bloque 1 as a formal-email writing task, shifting the survey's casual spoken questions into a formal written register.
Teacher notes & answer key
Bloque 1 — Guided 1 (situación → frase): No entiendo · ¿Cómo se dice ___? · Repite, por favor · Necesito ayuda · ¿Puedo ir al baño? · Buenos días. Sort (8): saludos/despedidas · pedir ayuda · frases de clase.
Bloque 2 — Completa la pregunta: ¿Cómo te llamas? · ¿De dónde eres? · ¿Dónde vives? · ¿Qué te gusta? · ¿Cuántos años tienes? · ¿Cómo estás?
Bloque 2 — Output (sample): Me llamo Ana. Soy de Miami. Tengo catorce años. Me gusta la música. Me gustan los deportes. (diagnostic — score for evidence, not accuracy).
Bloque 3 — Task design: gap type oral; structure sort → self-ID frames → partner interview → class circulation → synthesis; complication resource scarcity (one answer per classmate). Target grammar: interrogative word order + llamarse / ser / tener.
Bloque 3 — Criteria: asks for help / uses survival Spanish · answers personal questions · contributes to the class profile. Baseline — record it in a Class-Progress Record before building Week 2.
TBLT pipeline: generated through a four-gate teacher-supervised pipeline (design brief → main task → pre-task → reflection), with an independent pedagogical-quality check (Lee-Schell rubric) between generation and teacher review. This task converged at 20/24 with zero revision rounds.
Week 2Aug 17–21 · 3 bloques

Spanish I bridge: personal information

Can-do: Exchange personal details, likes, interests, and basic descriptions. New structures: ser + tener, then gustar + estar.

Task: mingle de intereses → class patternProduct: Todo sobre mí profileInterpersonal + WritingView full lesson →
  • Bloque 1

    Soy y tengo

    Puedo decir quién soy y qué tengo.

    Do-Now: spiral Wk1 (4)→Input: ser + tener + CFU→Guided 1: conjuga (6) · blocked→Encuentra a alguien que… (≥6)→Guided 2: interleave (6)→Output: ¿Quién soy? (4–5 sent.)→Reflexión: Wk1 task → correo formal
  • Bloque 2

    Me gusta y estoy

    Puedo decir qué me gusta y cómo estoy.

    Do-Now: spiral B1 (4)→Input: gustar + estar + CFU→Guided 1: escoge (6) · blocked→Entrevista: gustos + sentimientos (≥6)→Guided 2: interleave gustar/estar/tener (6)→Output (4–5 sent.)→Exit (3)
  • Bloque 3task

    Tarea: mingle de intereses → un patrón de la clase

    Puedo escribir un perfil corto y encontrar compañeros con un interés en común.

    Do-Now: spiral→Mingle info-gap → report one class pattern
Why: Bloque 1 encodes ser/tener blocked; Bloque 2 adds gustar/estar and then interleaves all four, so students must choose rather than repeat. The ser/estar contrast is deliberately held back to Week 3 — introducing a confusable pair before its first member is stable is how ser/estar goes wrong. Bloque 1 also opens with Week 1's reflection instead of its usual exit ticket: spaced across days, students write a formal email reporting the survey results, deliberately shifting register from the spoken task.
Teacher notes & answer key
Bloque 1 — Conjuga: Yo soy estudiante · Yo tengo catorce años · ¿Tú eres de Florida? · Mi amiga tiene el pelo largo · Él es simpático · Yo tengo una hermana.
Bloque 1 — Interleave: ¿Cuántos años tienes? · Yo soy de Miami y tengo dos hermanos · ¿De dónde eres tú? · Mi profesor es paciente · Ella tiene el pelo corto y es simpática · ¿Cómo te llamas?
Bloque 2 — Escoge: Me gusta el fútbol · Me gustan las clases de arte · Me gusta la música · Me gustan los deportes · Yo estoy contento/a hoy · ¿Cómo estás tú?
Bloque 2 — Output (sample): Me gusta la música. Me gustan los deportes. No me gusta la tarea. Estoy contento/a en la clase de arte. Estoy cansado/a en la clase de matemáticas.
Criteria: gives details (name, age, origin, ≥2 likes) · asks a follow-up during the mingle · reports one class pattern. Update the Class-Progress Record — note ser/estar readiness before the Week 3 contrast beat.
Week 3Aug 24–28 · 3 bloques

Identity and self — ser / estar

Can-do: Describe personality, appearance, emotions, identity, and interests. The year's first confusable pair: ser (traits) vs. estar (feelings).

Task: entrevista + resumen oralProduct: interview notes + spoken summaryInterpersonal + PresentationalView full lesson →
  • Bloque 1

    Ser (rasgos) y estar (sentimientos)

    Puedo escoger ser para quién soy y estar para cómo estoy.

    Do-Now: spiral Wk2 (4)→Input: side-by-side contrast + CFU→Guided 1: escoge ser/estar (6)→Entrevista (≥6 exchanges)→Guided 2: clasifica rasgo/sentimiento (8) · confusables adjacent→Output: auto-descripción (5 sent.)→Exit (3)
  • Bloque 2

    Apariencia y personalidad

    Puedo describir cómo es alguien con la concordancia correcta.

    Do-Now: spiral B1 (4)→Input: adjetivos + concordancia + CFU→Guided 1: concordancia (6)→Describe y adivina · info-gap (≥6)→Guided 2: interleave ser/estar/gustar (6)→Output: presentación oral (≥4 sent.)→Exit (3)
  • Bloque 3task

    Tarea: entrevista con seguimiento + resumen oral

    Puedo entrevistar a un/a compañero/a con preguntas de seguimiento y resumirlo/a en voz alta.

    Do-Now: spiral→Entrevista con preguntas de seguimiento → resumen oral
Why: ser/estar is the anchor confusable of the year, so it follows the rule exactly: introduce the new member blocked in Week 2 → teach the contrast as its own beat here → interleave with a sorting task that puts the confusables side by side. Bloque 2's output rotates to speaking — Weeks 1–2 were all written — to rehearse the oral summary the Bloque 3 task ends on.
Teacher notes & answer key
Bloque 1 — Escoge ser/estar: Yo soy creativo/a (rasgo) · Ella está nerviosa hoy (sentimiento) · Yo soy de Florida (origen) · Tú estás cansado/a · Mi amigo es simpático · Yo estoy contento/a hoy.
Bloque 1 — Clasifica (8): Rasgo (ser): alto · de Miami · trabajador · simpático. Sentimiento (estar): cansado · nervioso · contento · aburrido.
Bloque 2 — Concordancia: María es alta · Los chicos son trabajadores · Ana tiene el pelo largo · Mis amigas son graciosas · Carlos es bajo · Ella es inteligente (sin cambio).
Bloque 2 — Output (spoken, sample): Mi hermano es alto y moreno. Tiene el pelo corto. Es muy gracioso y trabajador. Hoy está contento porque le gusta el fútbol. Score the spoken turn, ≥4 sentences.
Criteria: asks 3 questions · answers with details (ser traits, estar feelings, likes) · summarizes the partner aloud. Then ask the diagnostic question: is ser/estar holding under interview load, or does it need to spiral into Week 4?
