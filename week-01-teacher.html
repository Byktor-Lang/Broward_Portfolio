Teacher Guide — Semana 1: Course launch & proficiency routines

Aug. 10–14 Spanish II · Miramar HS · Sr. Azuaje Modes: Interpersonal + Presentational 3 blocks (~90 min)

Week goal (from calendar): Use classroom Spanish, ask for help, complete a baseline speaking & writing sample. Spiral targets: greetings, question words, classroom commands (Spanish I activation — no class records yet, so scaffolding runs high and difficulty is calibrated down). Assessment: diagnostic survey + writing in Block 3 (score for evidence, not accuracy). Task count: one (Block 3); Week 1 is routine-building, so no second task.

Bloque 1 — Saludos & frases de supervivencia

Objective: I can greet someone, introduce myself, and ask for help in Spanish.

Mode: Interpersonal

Sequence + timing + volume (deep arc — 88 min)
1.Do-Now — activación (8) — 4 items: recall from Spanish I / summer (a greeting · say your name · a class word · count 1–5). No prior block to spiral yet.
2.Input + CFU (15) — 8-phrase survival bank, ≥3 modeled uses + 2 CFUs; model pronunciation, students echo.
3.Guided pass 1 — situación → frase (15) — 6-item set, controlled selection from the bank.
4.Communicative core — mingle (18) — ≥6 exchanges: greet 6 classmates, ask each for help with a different phrase, record names.
5.Brain break (3)
6.Guided pass 2 — clasifica (12) — 8-item sort into Saludos/despedidas · Pedir ayuda · Frases de clase (discrimination; no prior grammar to interleave on day 1).
7.Extended output — auto-presentación (12) — 4–5 sentence written self-introduction (greeting + name + origin + age + one thing you need help with).
8.Exit ticket (5) — 3 items: which survival phrase for each situation?

Extension (Reto extra): Add two more phrases you wish you knew; we translate them together.

Why: Survival language is the highest-leverage content of the year — it keeps the room in Spanish from day one. Lens: the activation Do-Now hooks low-stakes (everyone can list something); the mingle is the mid-block spike (up and talking on day 1); challenge varies written → spoken → sort → write; the self-intro paragraph is the payoff ending. Difficulty sits in band — recall, not production of new grammar.

Hard on purpose: the Do-Now asks for recall from memory (last year / summer), not a copy — expect thin, uneven lists. That is the baseline, not a failure.

Materials: Lesson §Bloque 1; projector for pronunciation model; timer for mingle; primary-source QR (short authentic Spanish self-introduction clip — attach your own).

Key — Bloque 1
Do-Now (activation — diagnostic, accept any reasonable recall): e.g. 1. Hola / Buenos días · 2. Me llamo… · 3. lápiz / profesor / clase · 4. uno, dos, tres, cuatro, cinco.
CFUs: (1) “You don’t understand — which phrase?” → No entiendo. · (2) “You need the word for something?” → ¿Cómo se dice ___?
Guided pass 1 (situación → frase): 1. No comprendes → No entiendo. · 2. Necesitas la palabra → ¿Cómo se dice ___? · 3. El profe habla muy rápido → Repite, por favor. · 4. Necesitas ayuda → Necesito ayuda. · 5. Permiso → ¿Puedo ir al baño? · 6. Saludas a la profe → Buenos días.
Guided pass 2 (sort, 8): Saludos/despedidas: Hola · Buenos días · Adiós · Hasta luego — Pedir ayuda: Necesito ayuda · No entiendo — Frases de clase: ¿Puedo ir al baño? · Repite, por favor.
Output (sample): Hola. Me llamo Ana. Soy de Miami. Tengo catorce años. Necesito ayuda con la pronunciación. (accept 4–5 sentences; diagnostic — do not grade spelling).
Exit ticket: 1. You don’t understand → No entiendo. · 2. You need the bathroom → ¿Puedo ir al baño? · 3. You need help → Necesito ayuda.
Bloque 2 — Palabras interrogativas & información personal

Objective: I can ask and answer basic questions about myself.

Mode: Interpersonal / Presentational

Sequence + timing + volume (deep arc — 88 min)
1.Do-Now — spiral (Bloque 1) (8) — 4 items: write the survival phrase for four situations.
2.Input + CFU (15) — 6 question words, each with a model question + answer + 2 CFUs.
3.Guided pass 1 — completa la pregunta (15) — 6-item set: insert the right question word.
4.Communicative core — entrevista (18) — ≥6 exchanges: ask a partner 6 questions, record answers; frames provided.
5.Brain break (3)
6.Guided pass 2 — empareja (interleave) (12) — 7-item question ↔ answer match, mixing Bloque 1 survival + Bloque 2 questions.
7.Extended output — Todo sobre mí (12) — 5-sentence self-profile (name, origin, age, 2 likes).
8.Exit ticket (5) — 3 items: answer 3 personal questions in full sentences.

Extension (Reto extra): Write one question you would ask a new exchange student.

Why: Question words unlock the year's interpersonal work — every task from here asks students to ask, not just answer. Lens: the spiral Do-Now hooks by retrieving Block 1; the interview spikes mid-block; challenge varies write → speak → match → write; the Todo sobre mí profile is the payoff and seeds Week 2's profile task.

Materials: Lesson §Bloque 2; interview partner assignments.

Key — Bloque 2
Do-Now (spiral): 1. No entiendo. · 2. ¿Cómo se dice ___? · 3. Necesito ayuda. · 4. Repite, por favor.
Question word → gloss: ¿Cómo? = how/what · ¿Qué? = what · ¿Dónde? = where · ¿De dónde? = from where · ¿Cuántos? = how many · ¿Cómo estás? = how are you.
Guided pass 1 (completa): 1. ¿Cómo te llamas? · 2. ¿De dónde eres? · 3. ¿Dónde vives? · 4. ¿Qué te gusta? · 5. ¿Cuántos años tienes? · 6. ¿Cómo estás?
Guided pass 2 (match, 7): ¿Cómo te llamas? → Me llamo Ana. · ¿De dónde eres? → Soy de Miami. · ¿Cuántos años tienes? → Tengo 14 años. · ¿Qué te gusta? → Me gusta la música. · ¿Dónde vives? → Vivo en Hollywood. · ¿Cómo estás? → Estoy bien. · ¿Necesitas ayuda? → Sí, no entiendo.
Output (sample): Me llamo Ana. Soy de Miami. Tengo catorce años. Me gusta la música. Me gustan los deportes.
Exit ticket: full-sentence answers, e.g. Me llamo… / Soy de… / Tengo … años.
Bloque 3 — Task block: class survey → class profile (TBLT)

Objective: I can share basic information about myself and contribute to the class profile.

Mode: Interpersonal + Presentational

Sequence + timing — TBLT nesting
1.Do-Now — spiral (Bloque 2) (8) — two question words + a question for each.
2.Pre-task (~17) — from the imported TBLT: prime the survey questions.
3.Main-task (~63) — from the imported TBLT: info-gap + class survey → build the shared class profile. Replaces input/practice/output. Duration-sized to fill the block, not the 45-min default.
4.Reflection — not here: opens Semana 2 · Bloque 1, replacing that block's exit ticket.
TBLT imported — info-gap class survey → class profile
Source: tblt-imports/week-01/ (rounds 1–3). Reskinned, content unaltered. Pre-task ~17 min + main-task ~63 min fill the block; the reflection opens Semana 2 · Bloque 1. Inspector: CONVERGED (Lee-Schell 20/24, 0 revisions).
Task at a glance
Gap type: oral — every student holds their own name / origin / age and needs classmates' answers to build the class profile; no one can complete Paso 5 alone.
Paso structure: categorization_interview_synthesis (sort → self-ID frames → partner interview → class circulation → synthesis).
Complication: resource_scarcity — the one-answer-per-classmate rule in Paso 4 rations the information, forcing circulation and help-Spanish rather than getting everything from one friend.
Target grammar: (1) interrogative word order qué / cómo / cuándo / dónde / de dónde / cuántos + verb; (2) present-tense self-ID verbs llamarse, ser, tener (me llamo, soy de, tengo … años).
Facilitation notes
Pre-task (~17 min): Warm-up 1 (ASSOC) is a silent brainstorm, then pair-share in a second color — don't pre-teach; it measures what they hold. Warm-up 2 (ODD) individual, then a quick whole-class "why" check. Warm-up 3 (MC bridge) circles the three question words, then each student says their three true answers aloud to a shoulder partner before writing. No classmate grid here — it primes the tools, it does not pre-solve the survey.
Paso 4 traffic: with 28 students, cap circulation at ~8 min and require standing up. The one-answer-per-person rule is what makes it a task, not a worksheet — enforce it.
Help-Spanish target (criterion 3): reward students you overhear using ¿cómo se dice…?, no entiendo, repita por favor, ¿puedo…? instead of switching to English.
Novice-Mid scaffold: keep the Paso 2 frames visible on the board while students interview — the model answer is the frame itself.
Pairing: rotate for Paso 4; don't re-pair the same two students from a previous TBLT cycle (class constraint).
Key — Bloque 3 (survey task)
Pre-task Warm-up 2 (odd one out + reason): 1. no entiendo (others are greetings/farewell). · 2. me llamo (others are question words). · 3. soy de (others are question words). · 4. tengo … años (others are help phrases).
Pre-task Warm-up 3 (MC bridge): 1. b) Cómo · 2. a) dónde · 3. b) Cuántos · 4. a) repita por favor · 5. b) ¿cómo se dice…?. Self-ID rehearsal lines are open — each student's own true name / origin / age.
Paso 1 (sort): Greetings → hola, buenos días, adiós. Asking for help → ¿cómo se dice…?, no entiendo, repita por favor.
Pasos 2 & 3 (open personal answers, accept any true response using the frame): ¿Cómo te llamas? → Me llamo + [name] · ¿De dónde eres? → Soy de + [place] · ¿Cuántos años tienes? → Tengo + [number] + años.
Paso 4 (survey grid): five distinct classmates, one answer each; grade for completeness and Spanish-only exchange, not spelling.
Paso 5 (synthesis): origin tally is correct if it faithfully sums the student's own Paso 4 grid; the "most common answer" line should match their highest count.
Design note — "likes" dimension deferred (Gate B)
The Spanish survey is scoped to name / origin / age (three fully-licensed questions — enough for criterion 1). The topic's "likes" dimension is deferred because gustar is out-of-fence for a Week-1 Novice-Mid diagnostic; adding a Spanish likes-question would require reopening the SCB. Documented, not a defect.

Extension (Reto extra): One sentence about the summer: “Este verano…”.

Why: Week 1's task is the class survey / info-gap → class profile (calendar). The Engine owns Blocks 1–2 (survival language + question words) and hands the task block to the imported TBLT — one major task cycle, the per-block cap.

Baseline diagnostic: collect the students' survey writing as the year's first writing sample — it seeds your first ZPD calibration. Record it in a Class-Progress Record.

Materials: Lesson §Bloque 3; the imported TBLT from tblt-imports/week-01; collected survey writing; primary-source QR.

Criteria — Bloque 3
Survey + profile (calendar criteria): asks for help / uses survival Spanish · answers personal questions (name, origin, age, likes) · contributes to the class profile. Score for evidence, not accuracy — this is the baseline.
After the week
Differentiation (Weekly Approval): the Lesson carries universal supports (word banks, frames). Decide per your roster whether any 504/IEP need calls for a separate Lesson Variant (reduced items / translated glossary) — a professional judgment, not a yes/no.
Write a Class-Progress Record from the diagnostic before building Week 2 — it sets the first real ZPD ceiling and the Week 2 Do-Now spiral.
