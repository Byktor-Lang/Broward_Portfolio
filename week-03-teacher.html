Teacher Guide — Semana 3: Identity and self

Aug. 24–28 Spanish II · Miramar HS · Sr. Azuaje Mode: Interpersonal 3 blocks (~90 min)

Week goal: Describe personality, appearance, emotions, identity, and interests. Spiral targets: Week 2 soy / tengo / me gusta. Interleaving status: this is the ser/estar contrast beat (Block 1) — teach the discrimination as its own beat and place confusables adjacent (ADR 0014); Block 2 interleaves description + feeling + likes. Reflection in: the Week 2 TBLT's reflection opens Block 1 (replaces its exit ticket) once imported — empty in this sample, so Block 1 runs its full arc. Assessment: partner interview + oral summary (Block 3). One task (ADR 0019).

Bloque 1 — Ser (rasgos) vs. estar (sentimientos)

Objective: I can describe personality and feelings with ser and estar.

Mode: Interpersonal

Sequence + timing + volume (deep arc — 88 min)
1.Do-Now — spiral (Wk 2) (8) — 4 items: retrieve me gusta / tengo / soy.
2.Input + CFU (15) — ser traits vs. estar feelings; ≥3 modeled each + 2 CFUs.
3.Guided pass 1 — escoge ser/estar (15) — 6-item set (trait vs. feeling).
4.Communicative core — entrevista (18) — ≥6 exchanges, frames provided.
5.Brain break (3)
6.Guided pass 2 — clasifica (interleave) (12) — 8-item sort: rasgo (ser) / sentimiento (estar), confusables adjacent.
7.Extended output — auto-descripción (12) — 5 sentences (ser + estar).
8.Exit ticket (5) — 3 items: label trait / feeling.

Extension (Reto extra): Write two more trait sentences about a character of your choice.

Why: ser/estar is the year's first confusable pair — teach the contrast as its own beat (ADR 0014); the spiral Do-Now is hard on purpose (ADR 0012). Lens (ADR 0017): the spiral Do-Now hooks; the interview is the mid-block spike; challenge varies written → spoken → sort → write; the exit self-labels for a payoff. Difficulty sits in band — the sort places confusables side by side so the decision is the point.

Hard on purpose: the spiral Do-Now retrieves three Week-2 structures cold before any new input (ADR 0012). The sort deliberately puts ser and estar items adjacent — expect hesitation; that hesitation is the discrimination forming.

Materials: Lesson §Bloque 1; primary-source QR (short authentic self-intro clip — attach your own); mini-whiteboards.

Key — Bloque 1
Do-Now (spiral): 1. me gusta · 2. Tengo · 3. Soy · 4. me gustan.
CFUs: (1) “personality trait — ser or estar?” → ser. · (2) “how you feel right now?” → estar.
Guided pass 1 (escoge ser/estar): 1. Yo soy creativo/a (rasgo) · 2. Ella está nerviosa hoy (sentimiento) · 3. Yo soy de Florida (origen) · 4. Tú estás cansado/a (sentimiento) · 5. Mi amigo es simpático (rasgo) · 6. Yo estoy contento/a hoy (sentimiento).
Guided pass 2 (sort, 8): Rasgo (ser): alto · de Miami · trabajador · simpático — Sentimiento (estar): cansado · nervioso · contento · aburrido.
Output (sample): Soy simpático/a. Soy trabajador/a. Soy de Miami. Hoy estoy contento/a. Estoy un poco nervioso/a por el examen.
Exit ticket: 1. Soy simpático = rasgo (ser) · 2. Estoy contento = sentimiento (estar) · 3. Soy de Miami = rasgo/origen (ser).
Bloque 2 — Apariencia y personalidad (concordancia)

Objective: I can describe what someone looks like and their personality, with adjective agreement.

Mode: Interpersonal / Presentational

Sequence + timing + volume (deep arc — 88 min)
1.Do-Now — spiral (Bloque 1) (8) — 4 items: a soy trait, an estoy feeling, ¿De dónde eres?, label one sentence rasgo/sentimiento.
2.Input + CFU (15) — descriptive adjectives (físico + personalidad) + agreement (‑o/‑a, plural ‑s); ≥3 modeled + 2 CFUs.
3.Guided pass 1 — concordancia (15) — 6-item set: make the adjective agree.
4.Communicative core — describe y adivina (info-gap) (18) — ≥6 exchanges: describe a person, partner identifies/draws; frames provided.
5.Brain break (3)
6.Guided pass 2 — interleave (12) — 6-item fill mixing description (ser) + feeling (estar) + gustar, confusables adjacent.
7.Extended output — presentación oral (12) — ≥4-sentence spoken turn: describe a friend/family member (appearance + personality + a feeling/like).
8.Exit ticket (5) — 3 items: choose the correctly-agreeing adjective.

Extension (Reto extra): Describe a character from a show with three adjectives, agreeing correctly.

Why: agreement is where description breaks down for novices, so it gets its own controlled pass before the info-gap. Lens (ADR 0017): the spiral Do-Now hooks and keeps the ser/estar contrast warm; describe y adivina is the spike (guessing = real info-gap); challenge varies agree → speak → interleave → present; the spoken presentation is the payoff and rehearses the Block-3 oral summary.

Hard on purpose: pass 2 puts es (trait) and está (feeling) in the same items — the just-taught discrimination now under load.

Materials: Lesson §Bloque 2; person/photo cards for the info-gap; recording device or presentation partners.

Key — Bloque 2
Do-Now (spiral, sample): Soy trabajador/a. · Estoy cansado/a. · Soy de Miami. · “Estoy nervioso” = sentimiento (estar).
CFUs: (1) “María is tall — alto or alta?” → alta. · (2) “two boys, hard-working — trabajador or trabajadores?” → trabajadores.
Guided pass 1 (concordancia): 1. María es alta. · 2. Los chicos son trabajadores. · 3. Ana tiene el pelo largo. · 4. Mis amigas son graciosas. · 5. Carlos es bajo. · 6. Ella es inteligente (sin cambio).
Guided pass 2 (interleave): 1. Mi hermana es alta y es simpática. · 2. Hoy yo estoy nervioso/a pero estoy contento/a. · 3. Me gustan las personas graciosas. · 4. Él es trabajador y hoy está cansado. · 5. Mi amigo es de Miami. · 6. ¿Cómo estás tú hoy?
Output (spoken, sample): Mi hermano es alto y moreno. Tiene el pelo corto. Es muy gracioso y trabajador. Hoy está contento porque le gusta el fútbol. (score the spoken turn; ≥4 sentences).
Exit ticket: 1. María es alta. · 2. Los chicos son trabajadores. · 3. Ana es graciosa.
